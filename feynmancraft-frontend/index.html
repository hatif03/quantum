What is a Feynman diagram?

Think of it as a map of a particle event. Lines are particles, junctions are interactions, and the shape tells a physicist which forces and conservation rules are involved. The diagram is not a literal photograph of the collision; it is a compact calculation blueprint.

The hard part is that good diagrams must satisfy both physics and LaTeX syntax. FeynmanCraft bridges those worlds: it explains the process, checks particle rules, then produces professional TikZ-Feynman code.
